OVERVIEW

About the Project

Art is a way people express themselves. Imagine looking at a time when art changed a lot. That is the Renaissance, a "rebirth" of art. This project aims to visually analyze Renaissance artworks, focusing on elements including color palette, culture, medium, and themes. While we'll analyze the Baroque and Rococo movements as extensions of the Renaissance style, the primary focus will remain on the Renaissance's art and artists. Understanding this time helps us see how people lived and offers insights into artistic expression throughout history.

Date

Tools

PROCESS

Data Cleaning & Preparation

To analyze artworks from various historical periods, I gathered data from The Metropolitan Museum of Art (MET) using open access CSV and MET API. Sorting the relevant artworks was challenging due to their diversity, but I finally narrowed it down by using specific search terms like 'Renaissance art' and a timeframe of 1400 to 1600 in Phyton. After refining the dataset in Excel, I visualized the data using R ggplot and transformed it into a cool infographic with Adobe Illustrator. While time-consuming, this process allowed for a comprehensive visual analysis of the artwork data.

Analysis Highlights

A color palette displaying how artists use color can be incredibly helpful for understanding the choices made by artists and the culture during that time. In Renaissance art, painters had a limited palette, using dark tones like brown, ochre, and gray for depth and warmth, and brighter colors like red, green, blue, and gold more subtly. I collected color data with hex codes in Phyton and used R's 'ggplot2' to create a visual palette for each period. Instead of varying sizes to show dominance, I opted for equal-sized color squares, suiting the project's theme and the final infographic's design.

I used R packages 'wordcloud2' and 'jiebaR' to create word clouds from artwork titles. This visually engaging approach conveys a lot of information in an accessible and memorable way. In Renaissance art, common words included religious figures like Child, Saint, Virgin, Christ, Madonn, Annunciation, and Angels. Portraiture-related terms like Portrait and Man were also frequent, along with words like Holy Family, Enthroned, and Young. Overall, word cloud is a creative and effective way to help viewers know the top themes of Renaissance art, which is about religion and portraiture.

I created pie charts using package ‘ggplot’ in R showing numeric data in the form of proportions. This chart for Renaissane art mediums shows the different mediums used by artists during Renaissance period. From it, it is clear to see that more than 50% of Renaissance artworks were created using oil as the primary medium.

INFOGRAPHIC ITERATION

UX Research

Before I came out with the final version of the infographic, I conducted UX research with individuals interested in art history or visual design. They provided feedback on the infographic's clarity, suggested improvements in areas like header and text color, and also introduced me to Figma for a more interactive design. This research led to a more user-friendly design, and after further refinement, the final infographic was created.
