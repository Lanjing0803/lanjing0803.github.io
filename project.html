Art in Renaissance, Baroque, and Rococo

PROGRAMMING | DATA ANALYSIS | DATA VISUALIZATION (PYTHON & R)

Art is a way people express themselves. Imagine looking at a time when art changed a lot. That is the Renaissance, a "rebirth" of art. This project aims to visually analyze Renaissance artworks, focusing on elements including color palette, culture, medium, and themes.

Pratt Connect, A Website Designed for Pratt Students

Concept to Prototype | User-Centered Design

At Pratt Institute, the School of Information community is diverse and from various walks of life. For graduate students, the primary focus is to build progressive connections for academic and career development. In the age of information overload, it is important to streamline the networks, build meaningful connections, and in turn facilitate the Pratt cohort.

Data Literacy with, for, and by Youth

NSF AWARDED PROJECT

DATA ANALYSIS | DATA VISUALIZATION (TABLEAU)

The goal of the Data Literacy with, for, and by Youth is to support data literacy programs for youth at the library. With the active participation of young people, the project aims to design, build, and test a prototype for youth-oriented data literacy activities, for use in after-school STEM programs at the public library.

SITES Website Usability Research

CLIENT: SMITHSONIAN INSTITUTE

USABILITY RESEARCH | WEBSITE RECOMMENDATIONS

The primary objective of the usability research conducted in this project was to assist SITES (Smithsonian Institution Traveling Exhibition Service) in enhancing their website and improving the overall user experience for their target audience. Multiple rounds of moderated user testing were carried out to identify areas for website improvement.

USABILITY RESEARCH | WEBSITE RECOMMENDATIONS

We’re Not Really Strangers is a brand that sells card games meant for people to get to know each other. The intention behind the brand is for players to forge meaningful connections with others. To improve the user experience(UX) on this website, our Usability Study Group utilized Unmoderated Remote User Testing to collect feedback from participants and generate recommendations.
